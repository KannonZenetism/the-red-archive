# Exotericism vs. Esotericism: A Study in Comparative Mythology

**Gerett Treas**
Murray State University
April 29, 2013

---

When approaching the topic of comparative mythology, one must keep an open mind. No one who adheres strictly to a single doctrine can adequately examine the various faiths and traditions to the full range of their potential. As we look at the various religions and spiritual practices throughout the ages, there is one rarely discussed topic that deserves to be examined and seriously contemplated: the distinction between the exoteric (the doctrine imparted to the general public) and the esoteric (that which is imparted to the initiated few). Underlying all faiths, at their root, is the foundation of occultism — the study of the secret, the mysterious, or the supernatural.

Occultism is widely denied and shunned by the various exoteric traditions of the world. In general — to America, and to American Christians in particular — *occult* is a dirty word. It is commonly associated with the dark arts and attributed to the workings of Satan. This, of course, like anything else, is a matter of personal interpretation. The only thing the occult means, as defined above, is that which is secret — or, more accurately, that which is hidden. The Holy Bible states, "The secret things belong to the LORD our God, but the things revealed belong to us and to our children forever" (Deuteronomy 29:29). Now, this might be taken as an excuse to say it is useless to search out the hidden things of God, because they belong to him; but the scriptures also teach, "For nothing is hidden that will not be made manifest, nor is anything secret that will not be known and come to light" (Luke 8:17).

The book of Daniel likewise teaches, "But you, Daniel, shut up the words and seal the book, until the time of the end. Many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall increase" (Daniel 12:4). It is a common misconception within the Christian Church that all revelation is complete. As one may discern from this verse, however — and from Luke 8:17, given above — there are hidden things that will be made manifest and unsealed as we approach the end time, or the end of the age, which is upon us now. As we move toward a more global civilization, as is happening every day, we draw closer and closer to the end. At the point when we reach a global government, with a global economy and a global religion, we will indeed have a global leader appointed over us — be he God or be he the devil.

What we will first do in this work is set forth the knowledge that has been lost, or rather hidden, throughout the ages, before we move into a more comparative study. The authors of *The Jesus Mysteries*, Timothy Freke and Peter Gandy, lay out the teachings of the exoteric and the esoteric as follows: "Each Mystery tradition had exoteric Outer Mysteries, consisting of myths, which were common knowledge, and rituals, which were open to anyone who wanted to participate. There were also esoteric Inner mysteries, which were a sacred secret known only to those who had undergone a powerful process of initiation. Initiates of the Inner Mysteries had the mystical meaning of the rituals and myths of the Outer Mysteries revealed to them, a process that brought about personal transformation and spiritual enlightenment" (Freke & Gandy, 1999, p. 4).

That being the case, we must ask ourselves why we have only the Outer Mysteries today. Why are the Inner Mysteries missing — or are they? The mysteries that remain are guarded, and have been guarded throughout the aeons, by powerful secret societies, some benevolent and some malevolent. There are some who hold the power of the mysteries and use it to manipulate the masses, in order to gain control over the population and to gain power and wealth for themselves. There are other secret societies, however, that passed their knowledge on orally, in secret, because they were persecuted and killed. The Gnostics are a perfect example of this. The mysteries were eventually eradicated when the Literalists (those who hold to a literal interpretation of scripture and take it as an account of literal historical events) aligned with the Roman Empire and gained the political power necessary to persecute the Gnostics. Naturally, being peaceful people, the Gnostics would not fight back. The Outer Mysteries were also more appealing to the masses, since they were more easily understood and required far less spiritual discipline than the Inner Mysteries demanded.

We will now turn to a comparative analysis of the Jesus character in relation to the mystery schools, and consider their interpretation of the matter. First, let us note that there is a collective figure in all the pagan mystery schools, some far predating the account of Jesus walking the earth. This character is a dying-and-rising godman who performed miracles, had twelve disciples, healed the sick, cast out demons, and accomplished all the other miracles commonly attributed to the one unique Literalist messiah; his collective name is Osiris-Dionysus. Manly P. Hall — 33rd-degree Freemason and occult philosopher of the twentieth century — had this to say of the Druidic tradition: "The Druids had a great number of feast days. The new and full moon and the sixth day of the moon were sacred periods. It is believed that initiations took place only at the two solstices and the two equinoxes. At dawn of the 25th day of December, the birth of the Sun God was celebrated" (Hall, 2003, p. 47).

"The birthday of Jesus is celebrated on the pagan festival of the returning sun on December 25" (Hall, 2003, p. 35). Naturally, given this information, we are not to assume that this date is unique to one man only; it is attributed to many ancient godman figures. Mithras, Attis, Bacchus, Zoroaster, Dionysus, Horus, Osiris, and Krishna, to name a few, are all said to have been born on December 25th and to hold many of the same divine attributes as Christ himself. The ultimate gist of the new-paradigm zeitgeist is astrotheology — the idea that all "Son" worship was actually, in origin, worship of the "Sun" in the sky. This may be a controversial topic for some who are conservative; but the dates show that it is at least worth taking into consideration, as these are among the most ancient teachings in the world.

Hall states: "Sun worship played an important part in nearly all the early pagan Mysteries. This indicates the probability of their Atlantean origin, for the people of Atlantis were sun worshipers. The Solar Deity was usually personified as a beautiful youth, with long golden hair to symbolize the rays of the sun. This golden Sun God was slain by wicked ruffians, who personified the evil principle of the universe. By means of certain rituals and ceremonies, symbolic of purification and regeneration, this wonderful God of Good was brought back to life and became the Savior of His people" (Hall, 2003, p. 42). One need not ponder long to see the dire importance of such revelations. Indeed, it would appear that the more modern tradition of "Son" worship may actually derive from an earlier, Atlantean "Sun" worship.

At this point it should be stated that the author does not wish to discredit the legitimate worship of God's Son, and is not in any way claiming that it is false. This is, however, a very scarcely recognized interpretation of events — one on which the author holds his own opinion — and it needs to be brought to light. Many ancient traditions are in fact misunderstood; and in order to be understood, and not branded as heretical, their doctrines must be set forth in an organized, intelligent, and fluent manner, thus showing that faiths which are not Christian are not necessarily demon-spawned Satan worshippers. The mystery schools are reemerging. This became apparent, to the best of the author's research, with the modern New Age Movement. This movement has caused the ancient mysteries to reemerge — Gnosticism, Kabbalah, Hasidism, Rosicrucianism — along with the knowledge of groups such as the Knights Templar, the Knights of Malta, the Order of the Golden Dawn, the Theosophical Society, Lucis Trust, and many, many more.

There are faiths that could be considered both exoteric and esoteric at the same time, since they practice both external rites and rituals and a kind of occult mysticism. In this respect we may mention the Hindu tradition, which would be more accurately termed Sanātana Dharma. Of this tradition we may say that its adherents worship God as Brahma. This God, like the God of many other faiths (hence the comparative mythology), is Trinitarian in nature. Many would not dare to draw such connections, but they are present nonetheless. The Hindu trinity consists of Brahma (the creator), Vishnu (the preserver), and Shiva (the destroyer). Of this, the Bhagavad Gita says: "It is both near and far, both within and without every creature; it moves and is unmoving. It is subtle, it is beyond comprehension. It is indivisible, yet appears divided in separate creatures. Know it to be the creator, the preserver, and the destroyer. Dwelling in every heart, it is beyond darkness. It is called the light of light, the object and goal of knowledge, and knowledge itself" (Easwaran, 2007, p. 218).

It is quotes such as this that would lead one to think a faith like Hinduism is both exoteric and esoteric in nature — namely, by saying that God is both "within and without every creature." The *within* may be recognized as esotericism, the *without* as exotericism. Before proceeding, we should elaborate a bit more on the Trinitarian aspects of the different faiths within comparative mythology. This is significant because it illustrates the underlying thread that may be traced through all faiths, if one looks beyond the external veils of separating words, cultural differences, and matters of interpretation. Were this done, it might be observed that they all speak of the same things, with only minor cultural dissimilarities. For example, the Sumerians had their trinity (the earliest in this round of cyclic existence, one which survives in the traditions of many indigenous peoples): Anu, Enki, and Enlil. The Egyptian trinity is Osiris, Isis, and Horus; the well-known Christian one, the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. In Gnosticism, the trinitarian godhead was Father, Mother, and Son; many of the various Mystery Schools held to an original triad of Spirit, Matter, and Chaos. Even Judaism, believe it or not — in all of its monotheistic glory — has a three-pronged faith after the order of its founders: Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

Buddhism, too, may be a form of both the esoteric and the exoteric — and in many ways it is. In fact, Buddhism is one half of the foundation of the modern New Age Movement, the other being Christianity. The New Agers hold that the Buddha is the Lord of Light and the Christ is the Lord of Love — hence the popular phrase "Love and Light." When one hears someone voicing New Age ideas say "Love and Light," they are actually saying "Buddha and Christ." The purpose of Buddhism is essentially to "go within" oneself to find peace and liberation — liberation from various bodily ailments, as well as from the bonds of the present physical form. In the book *World Religions: The Great Faiths Explored and Explained*, the origins of Buddhism are given as follows: "Siddhartha Gautama attained 'enlightenment,' the ultimate truth by which people are freed from the cycle of rebirth. He became the Buddha, meaning 'enlightened one,' who taught others the way to escape from the constant rebirth and therefore from suffering" (Bowker, 2006, p. 61). As the term "enlightenment" suggests, something rather otherworldly is being spoken of here. The attainment of enlightenment is the gaining of spiritual knowledge not previously possessed. It is quite funny how, traditionally, the Christian Church (at least in the author's experience) will say that there is no such thing as "enlightenment," and yet will say that one can come "out of the darkness and into the light." Both phrases refer to an unveiling, a revealing of things previously unknown, while one was still in the darkness of ignorance — for light is information, and darkness is ignorance. Now, while Buddhism is not esoteric in the initiatory sense (as Gnosticism or Rosicrucianism are), it is esoteric in the sense that only a few actually master it; not everyone is an enlightened being.

The question of why some people are aware of the internal (esoteric) teachings while others comprehend only the external (exoteric) ones may be answered by the fact that there are, quite literally, different types of people in this world. Some are, in their essence and being, spiritual people, while others are, in a quite literal sense, worldly. The Gnostics describe different levels of the human person, and different levels of consciousness within which people resonate. These are given in the scriptures of the Gnostic Bible: "Human beings are divided into three groups in Valentinian thought: hylics—material people of flesh and blood who are unbelievers; psychics—psychical people, people with soul, who are ordinary Christians; and pneumatics—truly spiritual people, people with divine spirit, who are gnostics. The last are the Valentinians themselves" (Barnstone & Meyer, 2009, p. 114). It may be noted that some schools of Gnosticism list a fourth type of person, following the pneumatic — simply the Gnostic, identified with the mystical identity, or the mystical person. It is extremely fascinating, enlightening, and wholly beneficial to contemplate this principle of varying degrees of the person; it explains so much. It marvelously illustrates why some people can only go so far, and why some can never penetrate the veil of Isis to behold the hidden mysteries of the inner sanctuary of God — for God is spirit, not flesh. To the hylic and the psychic, however, God is identifiable only with the flesh, for they cannot comprehend spiritual things.

The Holy Bible states, "God is spirit, and his worshipers must worship in the Spirit and in truth" (John 4:24). We shall now look at the difference between the esoteric and the exoteric within the Holy Bible itself, for it can certainly be found there, though it is little understood or recognized. There is indeed a dualistic nature within the Holy Scriptures (contrary to the mainstream exoteric church), and one may observe either that God himself is a duality, or that the principle of duality emanates from God. The Bhagavad Gita says this: "The states of sattva, rajas, and tamas come from me, but I am not them. These three gunas deceive the world: people fail to look beyond them to me, supreme and imperishable. The three gunas make up my divine maya, difficult to overcome" (Easwaran, 2007, p. 153). In this concept — not necessarily biblical, but recognizing the interconnectedness of all faiths — we may see that God is not necessarily the things that emanate from him, such as light and darkness; rather, he sends forth an illusion (the exoteric principle) that must be overcome (by the esoteric principle).

God is beyond all our worded abilities to comprehend, and therefore any title we might place on him (or her, or both, for that matter) would be an inadequate description, doing his actual character and nature no justice. God himself says this of his creative authority: "I form light and create darkness, I make well-being and create calamity, I am the LORD, who does all these things" (Isaiah 45:7). There is simply no way around this verse other than to acknowledge that the source of evil is God himself — especially since his being omniscient dictates that he knew evil would come into existence before he ever created it. Now, God may not be the darkness in himself (as in the Bhagavad Gita), but the text says that he is the source of darkness, for he "creates darkness." A common counterargument may be found in 1 John 1:5: "This is the message we have heard from him and proclaim to you, that God is light, and in him is no darkness at all." This may at first prove a bit confusing, and even seem to oppose the previous passage (which it does); but let us not forget that Christ himself ushered in a new covenant, starkly in contrast to that of the Old Testament. Again, the teachings of the Old and New Testaments are evidence of scriptural duality — for why would God manifest anything but his own nature: the darkness (the vengeance and wrath of the Old Testament) and the Light in which there is no darkness at all (the love and mercy of the New Testament)? Such is the nature of God, the source of all.

Another scripture that reveals the dual nature of God — and his Holy Bible as a means of discerning between spirit and flesh — is this: "For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart" (Hebrews 4:12). Notice all the dual, or doubled, phrases here. First we see the two-edged sword; then the phrases *soul and spirit*, *joints and marrow*, and finally *thoughts and intentions*. The scriptures are written in such a way as to judge the heart of the one who reads them; and that is why, if you think about it, I will dare to say there is even good and evil found within the Bible itself. Take the instance of God commanding the Israelites to kill men, women, and children in 1 Samuel 15:3, and contrast it with Jesus teaching us to "turn and become like children" (Matthew 18:3). We are given these differing views, in the author's opinion, to see how the heart reacts when we read such events. Are we accepting of the unrighteousness — or do we reject the idea that a loving God (or the loving aspect of a dualistic God) would desire anyone to kill an innocent child or sacrifice an innocent animal? It is not the rejection of certain aspects of Scripture that we will be judged by, but rather how accepting we are of the wrongdoing we encounter within it. The Holy Bible is a veiled mystery and an encoded document to those without eyes to see and ears to hear, imparting to us the eternal message of the hidden divine.

Any spiritually discerning fundamentalist Christian should recognize it for what it is: the fulfillment of biblical prophecy concerning a world religion, which will lead to the advent of the Antichrist in the last days. This spiritual system is, however, very appealing, and it offers many good, true, and pure doctrines. The New Age Movement is in fact an externalizing of the ancient mystery schools — those which the Bible calls "Mystery: Babylon the Great" (Revelation 17:5). The real question here is this: are they using these pure and appealing doctrines to lure extra-biblical seekers into a trap, or are they actually practicing what they preach? Of this we can only know in time — and that time is fast approaching, as the New World Order they (and many others) await is rapidly coming into place.

The transformation of an egocentric model of 'me and mine' into a world-centric mindset of 'we and ours' is the vessel that accommodates such a revolution of values, creating space for the emergence of a global citizenry" (Hubbard, 2012, p. 155). Words such as "global citizenry" and "globalization" should be taken very seriously by every discerning believer in an all-powerful God. Never before in the history of mankind have we had the technology to "gaze on" the bodies of two dead men lying in the public square of a great city. Of course, when speaking of any spiritual or religious matter, one inevitably comes to the topic of the origins of the universe. Here many schools differ (or do they?), namely on the amount of time creation has been in existence. Biblical creationism, for example, holds that the world and all its life were created around 6,000 years ago, tracing the biblical genealogies all the way back to Adam. Hinduism, in turn, holds that the universe is around 155.5 trillion years old and proceeds in phases called "cyclic existences," wherein civilizations rise and fall and ages come and go. A common comparative explanation is that the Bible begins near the advent of the Age of Taurus, an astrological house based on the sign of Taurus the Bull. This is why Moses was so incensed when the Israelites began to worship the Golden Calf after being led out of Egypt: by that time it was the beginning of the Age of Aries the Ram — hence God's calling out of Abram: *Ab-ram*. *Ab* means "father," and the ram is self-explanatory. This is why, to this day, the Jews still blow the ram's horn. It is also why Jesus made "fishers of men" and fed the multitude with "two fish," which is the astrological symbol for the Age of Pisces. A fuller examination of this would require a treatise on astrotheology, which is beyond the scope of the present work.

The creation story of Sumer is one of incredible interest, and a source from which many claim even the biblical writers derived their modified account of creation. It does not deal much with the creation of the world as we know it, but largely with the creation of man, which is attributed to beings the Sumerians collectively called the Anunnaki — translated, "those who from heaven to earth came." So these beings came from "heaven" — the skies, or outer space — and created man on earth. Zecharia Sitchin, the only man to have successfully deciphered the Sumerian clay tablets, states in his book *The 12th Planet*: "The Nefilim (fallen ones), it appears, came to Earth for gold and its related metals. They may also have come for other rare metals—such as platinum" (Sitchin, 1976, p. 326). It is said that the home planet of the Anunnaki, called Nibiru, was dying, and that the Anunnaki needed gold to replenish their atmosphere and ensure their planet's survival. This is why they came here. They found man in a primitive state, such as Neanderthal. Then, growing tired of their work mining for gold and the like, they decided to make a worker to save them from their labor. Enki — the Anunnaki's chief scientist and the second son of Anu, the head god of Nibiru — was said to have crossed the Anunnaki's own DNA with primitive man in order to create modern *Homo sapiens*. To the Sumerians, this was their origin. A contrast to this can, oddly enough, be found in the biblical account of creation: "The Nephilim were on the earth in those days—and also afterward—when the sons of God went to the daughters of humans and had children by them. They were the heroes of old, men of renown" (Genesis 6:4). It is commonly interpreted that the Nephilim in the biblical account, as well as the sons of God, were none other than the Anunnaki, which in Christian thought would be considered fallen angels. The genetic manipulation may well account for the destruction of the world by the flood, since the world was said to have been corrupt. It is an interesting fact that one of the definitions of *corrupt*, or *to corrupt*, is "to change the original form of." Now, if the Nephilim were genetically manipulating humans or other beings on the planet, this could be the origin of mythological creatures such as unicorns, centaurs, mermaids, fairies, and gargoyles. God could have destroyed the world partly because of this corruption.

In her book *Isis Unveiled, Vol. I*, H. P. Blavatsky states: "One of its illustrations represents the Divine Essence emanating from ADAM like a luminous arc proceeding to form a circle; and then, having attained the highest point of its circumference, the ineffable Glory bends back again, and returns to earth, bringing a higher type of humanity in its vortex. As it approaches nearer and nearer to our planet, the Emanation becomes more and more shadowy, until upon touching the ground it is as black as night…….The human body was of a half-ethereal nature; and that, before the fall, mankind communed freely with the now unseen universes. But since that time matter has become the formidable barrier between us and the world of spirits…….As the cycle proceeded, man's eyes were more and more opened, until he came to know 'good and evil' as well as the Elohim themselves. Having reached its summit, the cycle began to go downward" (Blavatsky, 2007, p. 4). Much more could be quoted from Blavatsky, but that would take more space than we have provided. It is obvious that she is speaking mysteriously about the biblical creation story, since it is an esoteric interpretation. She envisions humanity existing in a higher state, with far greater abilities than we currently have; and in that state we became increasingly aware of good and evil, and so "fell," as in the biblical fall. We then became carnal beings of flesh. What is not mentioned here is that we are on the path of ascent — moving back toward a state of ethereal existence. That day is rapidly approaching, when we will be changed in the twinkling of an eye.

The esoteric mysteries have, in the past, received so much hatred and persecution as to be wiped out and eradicated. Even today, the peaceful message of the spiritual interpretation is harshly oppressed. It is said that these practitioners are heretics, twisters of scripture worthy of rebuke, members of the synagogue of Satan, and any number of other such terms. But this is not so. Many of the mystery schools practiced strict vegetarianism or veganism — as does the author — and desired to harm no living being. If that is to be contrasted with the Literalists, who burned the bodies of the esoterics at the stake along with their women and children, then one must ask: which are the more righteous? It never fails that the Literalist tries to pick the esoteric apart, citing every little detail that has been omitted. Indeed, the esoteric is always laboriously wearied by the exoteric, who must be told everything many times over because they lack the ability to comprehend what has, in reality, already been given.

The final topic to explore will be the Jesus fish stories. While the fish means different things, only one of them will be covered here. It is the author's belief that the Messiah did not consume animal flesh: the only time the scriptures say Jesus ate fish was after the resurrection. He did not eat fish at the feeding of the four thousand or the five thousand. Note, too, that this is the only time the fish is mentioned as cooked — "broiled," to be exact (Luke 24:42). First we will look at the account of Jesus feeding the five thousand. The passage reads: "The disciples did so, and everyone sat down. Taking the five loaves and the two fish and looking up to heaven, he gave thanks and broke them. Then he gave them to the disciples to distribute to the people. They all ate and were satisfied, and the disciples picked up twelve basketfuls of broken pieces that were left over" (Luke 9:15–17).

Now, if you read the passage, you will see that he gave them to the disciples to distribute *to the people* — nowhere does it say that he ate any himself. This is the whole of the passage; then he speaks to his disciples privately. On top of this, the two fish are the symbolic spiritual food for the Age of Pisces, whose sign is "two fish," and the loaves of bread are the word of God: "I am the bread of life." And if you notice, twelve baskets were left over — the exact number of houses in the zodiac, and the number of Jesus's disciples: Jesus + disciples = "the Sun and the zodiac." It is an esoteric, astrotheological, analogical tale representing spiritual food for the Age of Pisces, nothing more. This is not to say that Jesus did not give these teachings literally, but it is a parable, an illustration — just the same as when he told the Jews they must eat his flesh and drink his blood to have eternal life. Surely we know he did not want them to eat him. It is the same with the fish.

Returning to Luke 24:42, let us add verse 43: "They gave him a piece of broiled fish, and he took it and ate it in their presence." At the very end of the Gospel (Luke 24:51), scripture says, "While he was blessing them, he left them and was taken up into heaven." Remember the blessing of the loaves of bread and the two fish? Again he blesses here. If one reads the whole account, one may easily discern that this was Jesus claiming his work was finished — done. That is why the fish was not mentioned as cooked until after the resurrection, after his work was completed. Recall Christ saying to his mother, "Woman, why do you involve me?… My hour has not yet come" (John 2:4). It is the same here. At the end of this Gospel, before his ascent, it was said of Jesus's disciples, "Then he opened their minds so they could understand the Scriptures" (Luke 24:45).

This is what I am attempting to do for you. There are deep lessons and mysteries hidden in the scriptures. The feeding of the multitudes and the eating of the broiled fish before his ascent are deeply significant spiritual teachings that Jesus left — not accounts of Jesus eating innocent animals. God did not intend us to eat animals, for God ordained the plants of the ground and the grains of the earth as our food; and for the lawful man, there is no other sustenance for the body. "Then God said, 'I give you every seed-bearing plant on the face of the whole earth and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be yours for food'" (Genesis 1:29). This is what God intended. Everything that follows from departing it is sin, and the result thereof.
