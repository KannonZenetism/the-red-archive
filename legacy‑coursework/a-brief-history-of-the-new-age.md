## Introduction

When we speak of the New Age movement, we are using an umbrella term; the terms *New Thought* and *New Spirituality* may be used interchangeably. There is much more to the movement than what we see today on the surface. This is, in fact, a movement of great antiquity. Some would call it a religion, but it is really a primeval spiritual system in the lineage of the ancient Magi and the Brotherhood of the Snake. More than a religion, it is a movement — and it has always been a movement toward the common destiny of the Order of the Quest: the quest to erect a shrine of Universal Light.

This movement, however, as all things do over time, has degenerated from its original form, becoming crossed and mixed, with much lost to its modern practitioners. Admittedly, many initiates who have guarded the sacred truths have confessed that the modern Mystery schools have lost much of the original meaning of their inner symbolism, leaving them with many symbols for which they have no key to unlock the meaning. In this work we will examine the history of this movement, how it emerged in America, and its projection for the future. Has this tree sprung from the Tree of Life, or did it emerge from the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil?

## American Roots of the New Age

We begin by quoting one of the progenitors of modern externalized New Age teaching, a figure especially important in the American lineage — keeping in mind that each individual covered in this work, like individuals in any discipline, varies in opinion concerning the truth and goal of the organizations they represent. Denominations, sects, and separatist groups of all sorts will continue to exist until the great day. As Manly P. Hall writes:

> In America shall be erected a shrine to Universal Truth, as here arises the global democratic Commonwealth — the true wealth of all mankind, which is designed in the foundation that men shall abide together in peace and shall devote their energies to the common cause of discovery.… The power of man lies in his dreams, his visions, and his ideals. This has been the common vision of man's necessity in the secret empire of the Brotherhood of the Quest, consecrated to fulfilling the destiny for which we in America were brought into being. (Hall, 2011)

What one takes from this will undoubtedly depend on one's own view of what these words require. Who was Manly P. Hall, and why should we listen to him? Hall wrote over 200 books and gave thousands of lectures around the world during his lifetime. He was a 33rd-degree Freemason and was considered the esoteric philosopher of the twentieth century, as well as one of the most credible men who ever lived in regard to esoteric mythology and lore. His students often cited his encyclopedic memory and his ability to photographically recall and apply nearly every new piece of information he acquired.

We must now define what we mean by the terms *New Age*, *New Thought*, and *New Spirituality*. The term *New Age* refers to a collective group of individuals who generally meld the teachings of East and West, and who await what may be called the dawn of the Age of Aquarius — an astrological age consisting of the precessional houses of the zodiac. The zodiac existed long before the Greeks, regardless of simplistic arguments to the contrary; it was established and taught by the Sumerian god Enki as early as 4,000 B.C.

We may define the Age of Aquarius as an astrological era expected to bring increased spirituality and harmony to earth. This Aquarian Age — the age of the Water Bearer — is represented by the image of a man with a jar of water, pouring it out from the vessel, which signifies the pouring out of the spirit upon the earth. Many believe this is even spoken of in the Holy Bible: "As you enter the city, a man carrying a jar of water will meet you. Follow him to the house that he enters" (Luke 22:10, NIV). The house he enters, many believe, refers to the zodiacal House and Age of Aquarius, following the Age of the two fish (Pisces) represented by Jesus the Christ.

*New Thought* is often seen as a term following *New Age*, since many believed the dawning of the Age of Aquarius had failed (though it is properly understood that we are in a transition between ages). New Thought can be synonymous with New Spirituality, as both refer to new methods of thinking compared with those revealed to the public before them. Some elementary notions of New Thought, as we have defined it, are these: that Infinite Intelligence, or God, is everywhere; that spirit is the totality of real things; that true human selfhood is divine; that divine thought is a force for good; that sickness originates in the mind; and that right thinking has a healing effect. The term is in some sense self-explanatory. It is new as regards its approach to syncretic thinking and its lack of the rigid doctrinal structure found in traditional religions. In truth, as already noted, it is actually the ancient way of thinking; but as far as the general public is concerned, and as far as we are defining religion as it is currently known, this is indeed a new way of thinking.

Alice A. Bailey is a woman many consider to be the mother of the modern New Age Movement. Her husband, Foster Bailey, was also a 33rd-degree Freemason — so there appears to be a pattern of connection between Masonry and what we are considering the religion, or spiritual movement, of the New Age. Speaking on the rites of initiation (the schools of antiquity did require one to be initiated), which some of the more paranoid stress as something horrible, Bailey had this to say regarding its nature:

> The word Initiation comes from two Latin words, *in*, into; and *ire*, to go; therefore, the making of a beginning, or the entrance into something. It posits, in its widest sense, in the case we are studying, an entrance into the spiritual life, or into a fresh stage in that life. It is the first step, and the succeeding steps, upon the Path of Holiness. Literally, therefore, a man who has taken the first initiation is one who has taken the first step into the spiritual kingdom, having passed out of the definitely human kingdom into the superhuman. (Bailey, 1922)

So initiation does not really mean some secret rite that no outsider may partake in (though that may be part of it); it really means embarking upon the spiritual path of transformation prepared for all true seekers of the way.

It is interesting to note that Alice A. Bailey, the mother of the modern New Age Movement, along with her predecessor H. P. Blavatsky — often called the grandmother of the modern New Age Movement — were both female (though there were important male figures as well). The New Age spiritual system is one based strongly on the concept of the Divine Feminine: the feminine aspect of God, that is, the Goddess. The Divine Feminine can be considered many things — the female aspect of God, Mother Nature, or the receptive and passive aspect of the energetic-cosmological body, complementary to the opposing polarity of the male, vital, active principle. The feminine is the receptive and passive principle in the sense that the masculine (spirit) acts upon the feminine (matter) to produce our physical universe (the child of the two), and it means still other things to other cultures, peoples, and traditions.

It is fitting to presume that one of the primary reasons for the large number of female adherents to the New Age is the rigid teaching concerning women's rights found within the Christian churches. It is also a reason for the rise of Christian Feminism — which, if we are honest, is also a New Age system of thought. In the New Age institutions, women no longer have to take a back seat; they are now seen as restoring the Goddess's rightful place on earth and in the cosmos, ridding the world of much male patriarchal domination and replacing it with the empathetic and shared female characteristics of nature and nurture. We may come under fire for claiming that females are more nurturing; however, in the universal scheme, as we are interpreting and defining it, nurture is a feminine trait — though certainly males can possess a nurturing nature, as well as other feminine characteristics.

Another prominent founder and early figure of the movement was Edgar Cayce. Cayce was thought of as an American psychic, able to answer various questions on issues as diverse as healing and ancient Atlantis. He also spoke on reincarnation and the Akashic Records, which caused many in his day — especially practicing Christians — to question his ideas about God and spirituality. Cayce made many predictions about the future, including a coming pole shift, the darkening of the sun, the emergence of new fields of science, and more. He also spoke of root races, which are essentially gene pools in the human family, and predicted that a fifth root race was coming, from which a new kind of human would emerge — the next evolution of our species.

The tension between these two groups — at least between those awake enough to know of the presence and purpose of the other — is astronomical, to say the least. Many Christian eschatologists believe that the New Age movement is the long-awaited World Religion described in Revelation, and that it will lead to the worldwide worship of the Antichrist (more accurately, the Beast): "The second beast was given power to give breath to the image of the first beast, so that the image could speak and cause all who refused to worship the image to be killed" (Revelation 13:15, NIV). Though there are many metaphorical ways to interpret this image of the beast, most mainstream Christians insist that it refers to a literal, physical figure in the future who will impose upon, and demand worship from, all the peoples of the world.

An example of a common view promoted by the mainstream Christians may be observed in the statements of one of its most outstanding advocates, Texe Marrs. Here is a brief statement of his that helps shed light on how this group tends to view the New Age movement:

> After many months of intensive study and exhaustive research of the New Age World Religion, its leaders, and their intentions, I have been able, with the help of Almighty God, to uncover a plot on the part of Satan of truly monstrous proportions. Shockingly precise and brutally frank in its aims, the dark dimensions of this plot stagger the imagination. The Kingdom of the Antichrist is at hand — make no mistake about it — and soon, very soon, the whole world will feel the savage impact of the Beast with the fearful number: 666. (Marrs, 1988)

It is quite easy to see that Texe Marrs is not fond of the New Age system; in fact, he believes it to be the very religion of the devil. This is so for a number of reasons, but mainly because the New Age rejects the regulations of modern Christianity in favor of broader ideas. Texe Marrs is a fundamentalist, make no mistake about it. Broader does not necessarily mean better, but it does mean that this movement will attract different types of people. In general, those looking for something new are fed up with traditional religion. Most of a mind similar to Texe Marrs's see no way for this to fit into Bible prophecy other than as some sinister plot — which, if we keep an open mind, we can admit it could be. The orthodox and the mainstream are highly identified with their position and carry an in-group bias.

Another prominent spokesperson and pastor who speaks on eschatological issues is Pastor Billy Crone. Crone's recent ministerial series, *The Final Countdown*, covers a variety of topics concerning the biblical End Times — among them the Jewish people, worldwide upheaval, modern technology, world government, world economy, and world religion. In Part 1 of the first sermon of the World Religion portion of the series, Crone says:

> And the 1st way we know we're really headed for A One World Religion is due to a Welcoming of All Faiths. That's right, I'm talking about the Interfaith Movement. You see, put yourself in the Antichrist's shoes. If you're going to deceive people into creating A One World Religion then you've also got to get rid of any sense of one religion being superior over another, right? Now, if we are to be honest, this does sound a bit elitist. One religion really is not superior to another, just as one person is not superior to another, nor any life form superior to another. It is quite plausible to think that the teaching of the enemy could be the superiority of one religion over another — a way to keep humanity in a perpetual state of conflict and to keep its mind off holy things, deterred by meaningless battles over names, places, and events.

We should also, in fairness, entertain the idea that this eschatological perspective is true. Figures like Deepak Chopra have said things such as:

> The first Jesus was a rabbi who wandered the shores of northern Galilee many centuries ago…he was the keeper of deep mysteries…This historical Jesus has been lost, however, swept away by history. Millions of people worship another Jesus, however, who never existed…This is the Jesus built up over thousands of years by theologians and other scholars…This second Jesus cannot be embraced without embracing theology first…Not the savior, not the one and only Son of God. Rather, Jesus embodied the highest level of enlightenment…The third Jesus offers a relationship that is personal and present…The third coming — finding God-consciousness through your own efforts. It is quite easy to see how this might offend many Christians. It is, in many ways, claiming that what they believe is false — even if the claims are true. The New Age as a whole denies the uniqueness of the divinity of Christ, claiming that he is one of many enlightened masters who have attained the Cosmic, or Christ, Consciousness throughout history. To the New Ager, it is Christ's level of consciousness that makes him distinct from the rest of humanity — not his supernatural birth, death, or resurrection, nor the assertion that he is the only begotten Son of God. In fact, most New Agers will reduce much of Christ's story to allegorical terms illustrating how consciousness has been nailed to the cross of matter — crucified — so that it may, by a process of initiation, be resurrected and ascend into the heavenly spheres (higher dimensions, or levels, of consciousness).

## Ancient History of the New Age

The antiquity of the New Age is not hard to prove if one is able to look past classifications and names, as the Mystery schools themselves have done throughout history. Most people simply believe it to be a modern movement and often see it as a mere rebellion against orthodox religion, started by flower children in the sixties who were influenced by traveling gurus from the east. But the roots of this movement are not hard to ascertain if we actually compare the precepts of the underlying doctrines, do a little comparative study, and apply some spiritual discernment to the issues raised by its propagators. When properly observed, one can see that the New Age is nothing more than a modern, externalized version of the ancient Mystery schools — the Magi, the Pythagoreans, the Alchemists, and the like — and is really, truth be told, a melding of all of these. This does not have to be viewed as a bad thing; of course, how it is viewed depends upon the person and their spiritual perspective.

In the times preceding Alice A. Bailey and Edgar Cayce, H. P. Blavatsky emerged and created the Theosophical Society, which is dedicated to preserving the ancient Mysteries and revealing what has been appropriate to reveal at certain times along the way. Blavatsky had two main works: one was *Isis Unveiled*, and the other, her magnum opus, *The Secret Doctrine*. The Mystery schools have a very different view of how the world came to be and where it is going. For the most part they hold that we are in a state of involution — a spreading out from Source (God) — and that true evolution is a movement back toward Source. In *Isis Unveiled*, Blavatsky describes the period of the fall of man, the beginning of the process of involution from Source, as follows:

> One of its illustrations represents the Divine Essence emanating from ADAM like a luminous arc proceeding to form a circle; and then, having attained the highest point of its circumference, the ineffable Glory bends back again, and returns to earth, bringing a higher type of humanity in its vortex. As it approaches nearer and nearer to our planet, the Emanation becomes more and more shadowy, until upon touching the ground it is as black as night…at the beginning, the human body was of a half-ethereal nature; and that, before the fall, mankind communed freely with the now unseen universes. (Blavatsky, 2012)

Such a view — as the mention of ADAM suggests — may be held as a non-traditional explanation of well-known theological concepts. It lends itself to the idea that humanity, or ADAM, once existed in an altogether different state. Yet upon touching the ground (earth), the procession — representative of the fall — becomes black as night. Here lies the fall of man, having fallen from a heightened spiritual state into a world of dark and gross matter. This is likewise the meaning of the clothing with the coats of skins found in the biblical accounts: "When the arc attained a certain point which brought it parallel with the fixed line of our terrestrial plane, the man was furnished by nature with 'coats of skin,' and the Lord God 'clothed them.'" So, in this view, when a certain involutionary stage was reached, humankind became physical and was garbed with a body of flesh. No living being was killed for clothes — it ought to seem absurd that a loving God would kill because someone sinned in the first place, thereby making a sinner of himself as well. Rather, it was a symbol for the procuring of a carnal nature. Man became flesh.

But even before figures like Blavatsky, Bailey, and Cayce, there were the pervasive Mystery schools that have always existed. Most of them trace their roots back to the time of Atlantis, when the knowledge of the Mysteries was passed to them by the gods. Some, such as the Kabbalists, teach that the angels relayed the Mystery teachings to Adam after he had been expelled from the Garden (the heightened state of existence), so that Adam, Eve, and their progeny might find their way back to that heightened state, and so that the human enterprise, through the knowledge of God, would eventually attain liberation and free creation from the curse Yahweh had set upon it. Some schools of the Mysteries, such as the Freemasons, trace their lineage to Egypt, but it may reach as far back as Babylon — for the Tower certainly needed builders.

In addition, groups such as the Gnostics, whose origins are placed around 100 A.D., do not actually begin on the dates given them. What modern historians fail to understand is that, by the way the Mysteries work, these organizations change form like a chameleon, like an actor who wears different suits for each part he plays. The Mystery schools are masters of guerrilla tactics and cloaked secrecy. This does not mean they are evil, as some have claimed. Granted, there is good and bad in everything on this planet, since we are neither fully God (good) nor fully devil (evil). Many of the Mystery schools became hidden because of oppression and persecution, forced underground under threat of death should they refuse to renounce their faith and practice. Like any rational being, they were not willing to surrender their sacred beliefs easily, so they never relinquished their faith — and many died for it. Yet these Mysteries are not lost to us. They have been preserved, awaiting a day when they may be brought back to the world anew, and even built upon for the increasing knowledge and continual revelation of today.

The Gnostic Gospel of Thomas illustrates the purpose of the Mysteries well, when Jesus says: "I speak my mysteries to those [who are worthy of my] mysteries. What your right hand does, let not your left hand know what it does." Jesus indicates that, just as in the Holy Bible, his teachings are not revealed to everyone. He spoke to the crowds in parables, explained those parables to the disciples, but allowed only his three inner-circle disciples — Peter, James, and John — to see things no one else could see. This mirror imagery appears elsewhere in the Bible. One such example is the Temple of Solomon, with its Outer Court, Inner Court, and Holy of Holies, representing ever-deepening levels of spirituality and closeness to God.

We will now move into some discussion of the primary tenets and beliefs of the New Age, and of how they differ from anything previously experienced in America — exoterically, that is. One very distinctive thing about the New Age is that it is a highly syncretic spiritual system, drawing from a variety of religious sources all over the globe. Its eclectic practices merge not only dogma and creed but also ritual and sacred space, all the while keeping things uniquely its own. Primarily it is a fusion of eastern and western traditions, making it a faith of truly global proportions, merging boundaries that were once divided by exclusivist dogmas and creeds. At this point the author would like to state that he is neither promoting nor demeaning the New Age movement, but reporting as his understanding allows him at the present time.

A core belief that shows a uniquely — but not solely — eastern influence is the doctrine of reincarnation: the rebirth of a soul in a new body. Buddhists, for example, believe that one can be reborn as an animal in a future life, and they hold that it is a privilege to be born human — though some contention may be held with the latter portion of that position. Reincarnation is not simply a notion of rebirth, but a notion of the immortal soul being transformed into another creature in a future life.

Hindus also believe in reincarnation. Like Buddhists, they believe in karma, which they feel is not organized or controlled by God; it is simply a principle of the accumulation of dues for actions performed in a lifetime, or across successive lifetimes. Reincarnation is ultimately about the cycle of birth and rebirth, a continual process, even though we may not remember it in this lifetime. Hindus believe that our desires keep us on the wheel of reincarnation, and that we must free ourselves from our desires to merge with the One. Likewise, the Kabbalists, or mystic Jews, also believe in reincarnation, and they too have a scriptural basis for it. When the scriptures state that God is "visiting the iniquity of the fathers on the children, to the third and the fourth generation" (Numbers 14:18, ESV), what they are saying, according to the Kabbalists, is that the children are the father of the sin, and that they will be granted three or four lifetimes to set things right.

Another distinctive belief held by those in the New Age movement is the belief in Ascended Masters, who, they claim, have guided humanity for millennia from behind the scenes. Indeed, New Agers claim that Jesus himself was one of these spiritual masters, but not the greatest of them. When he walked the earth, the Master Jesus, as they call him, was overshadowed by Lord Maitreya, the coming and final avatar for humanity. Maitreya will be the long-awaited figure awaited by every religion: the second coming of Christ, the Jewish Messiah, the Maitreya Buddha, Lord Kalki to the Hindus, Pahana — the True White Brother of the Hopis — and so on. This raises the question of the future impact this will have on the world's religions, which we will come to shortly.

This issue is important because there is a major factional divide between the New Agers and those on the Christian side who believe these Ascended Masters are fallen angels masquerading as angels of light, as the scriptures call Satan and his hosts. This may well be supported, if by nothing else, by the act of invocation, which literally means the summoning of a deity or the supernatural. It is the author's personal belief that if the Creator of the universe wishes to communicate with someone, he will come to them, and they will know it. Deity does not need to be summoned. There is no magic button for God, and Deity is not an errand boy for human beings.

The Gnostics, who were a major influence on the New Age — and on other Mystery religions as well — believe something very different about what they term the Archons. Is this another example of the New Agers falling from the eternal truths? The Gnostics believe that the former of this material world is what is called a Demiurge. The Demiurge is merely a former of worlds, not the Prime Creator. Likewise, the helpers of the Demiurge, or angels, are called Archons, and they are comparable to the Fallen Angels, the Ascended Masters, and the gods and goddesses in the pantheons of every major cultural religion in the history of the planet. Of the Archons and their ruler, the Gnostics say, in *The Hypostasis of the Archons* (also known as *The Reality of the Rulers*):

> Their chief is blind; because of his power and his ignorance and his arrogance he said, with his power, "It is I who am God; there is none apart from me." When he said this, he sinned against the entirety. And this speech got up to incorruptibility; then there was a voice that came forth from incorruptibility, saying, "You are mistaken, Samael" — which is, "god of the blind."
>
> His thoughts became blind. And, having expelled his power — that is, the blasphemy he had spoken — he pursued it down to chaos and the abyss, his mother, at the instigation of Pistis Sophia. And she established each of his offspring in conformity with its power — after the pattern of the realms that are above, for by starting from the invisible world the visible world was invented.

Following this account, the creation of man is given: "The rulers laid plans and said, 'Come, let us create a man that will be soil from the earth.' They modeled their creature as one wholly of the earth." These verses give us a very different picture of events from the one in Genesis and the Christian tradition. It is well known that the word for God used in the first chapter of Genesis is *Elohim*, meaning gods (plural) — both male gods and female goddesses. The chief of these gods, as shown above — who is Yahweh in the biblical account — is not the first cause; he and his legion are those who create and form, yet are not themselves first causes.

The Book of Enoch does not state it in exactly this way, in that it does not regard God, as seen in the Old Testament, as the ruler of the Archons, the fallen angels. Rather, Samyaza (Sêmîazâz) is the chief of the fallen angels — who in comparative mythology is Satan — and his chief agent is Azazel (Azâzêl), who is Lucifer in the comparative mythos. The Book of Enoch describes the descent of the Fallen, or the Archons, as follows:

> Then sware they all together and bound themselves by mutual imprecations upon it. And they were in all two hundred; who descended ⌈in the days⌉ of Jared on the summit of Mount Hermon, and they called it Mount Hermon, because they had sworn and bound themselves by mutual imprecations upon it. And these are the names of their leaders: Sêmîazâz, their leader, Arâkîba, Râmêêl, Kôkabîêl, Tâmîêl, Râmîêl, Dânêl, Êzêqêêl, Barâqîjâl, Asâêl, Armârôs, Batârêl, Anânêl, Zaqîêl, Samsâpêêl, Satarêl, Tûrêl, Jômjâêl, Sariêl. These are their chiefs of tens.

In the Enochian account, the angels swore to one another by oath to be bound by what they had done. It was a mutual agreement, in that they knew they were rebelling against the higher forces. Some of a more New Age cast of thought may hold that this is all symbology, not actual beings performing these actions. Many esoteric thinkers regard a great deal of these accounts — including what happens in the Holy Bible — as allegorical and metaphorical expressions of the manifestations and variations of consciousness. Consciousness fell by mutual bond when primeval man rebelled against the higher principles.

## The Future and the New Age

At this point it is appropriate to discuss the implications of the New Age for the future, especially as it relates to America and the prophetic opposition it receives from Christianity. The New Age calls for globalization and the minimization of otherness, such as is seen in the separatist attitude of other religions. Here are the goals listed by the leaders of the New Age: (1) a new world order; (2) a universal credit card system; (3) a world food authority; (4) a world health authority; (5) a world water authority; (6) a universal tax; (7) a universal military draft; (8) the abolishing of Christianity, Judaism, and Islam; and (9) one world leader (Cumbey, 1983). It is worth noting that goal (8) is incomplete. The New Age wants no religion but all religion — and that means no Hinduism, no Jainism, not even Secular Humanism, for that matter. They want one world faith to go along with one world leader in one world system, period.

Given that this is the plan, it is observable that this abolition of otherness ends in the abolition of those who are other, making the very ones who devised the plan other themselves. In other words, they are guilty of what they accuse others of being guilty of. However important oneness is to the individual and to the global family, when one goes so far as to say that those who are other are worthy of annihilation, it is being taken too far. If the New Agers are truly as much about love and light as they claim, then killing in the name of unity cannot be enacted. Love does no harm. Love is selfless, hoping only for the light to shine upon those in darkness. Darkness, however, is destruction. Darkness is chaos and mayhem — not order, not peace. With that said, their leaders are known to call themselves the rider on the pale horse, whose name is death. They believe they are the death-bringers of God, and this could become very dangerous. It must be considered as we move forward with the development and reinterpretation of ancient precepts in the twenty-first century.

We will now look at one of the major prophecies in Revelation concerning the advent of a World Leader who heads a World Religion, as interpreted by mainstream Orthodox and Protestant Christians: "The second beast was given power to give breath to the image of the first beast, so that the image could speak and cause all who refused to worship the image to be killed" (Revelation 13:15, NIV). Upon this and other verses rests the belief in a coming false prophet for a false World Leader who heads a false World Religion, whose image is to be worshipped under the looming threat of death. Revelation does not paint a pretty picture when it comes to the goals and forcefulness of the Beast, whom many call the Antichrist — though no such word is used for this figure in Revelation.

How much of this is paranoia? The author has, in the past, been an advocate of such a worldview, believing the time of the return of the Christ to be quite near. And while he still believes such an advent is imminent, the interpretation and unriddling of these prophecies are now open to broader interpretation and debate. The pointing of fingers by one side is no better than the pointing of fingers by the other — if indeed there are such things as sides to begin with. While we must admit that such New Age aspirations do fit the prophetic scheme of the Christian tradition, they also fit the prophetic scheme of every other religion on the planet, which is what makes this period in history so interesting. We are staged to see the greatest divine spectacle ever witnessed by human beings — Armageddon and the Second Advent of Christ (however we may define the function and person of that title).

The Buddhists await the fifth Buddha; the Hindus await the tenth avatar; the Muslims await the Imam Mahdi; the Hopi peoples await the True White Brother; the Jews await the Messiah (since they rejected Jesus when he came); and the Christians await the Second Advent of Christ. Some of these prophecies are eerily similar. The Hindu tenth avatar, for instance, comes riding a white horse bearing a sword, and reigns for a thousand years on earth — exactly like Jesus. Both the Hindu avatar and the next Buddha come at a time when Dharma is lost — that is, when God's laws and precepts are forgotten and the world is at its worst — exactly as in Revelation, when the world is primed for the arrival of the Antichrist (the Beast): a time when the world is in a state of utter servitude and control, enslaved to the Beast system (a lower level of material consciousness).

There are some modern New Age Christian organizations as well, such as Christ Universal Temple. Some have pointed out that its founder, Johnnie Coleman, was too enamored of wealth, to which she responded that wealth can be an instrument God uses to spread the Gospel throughout the world. This organization is actually a perfect example of New Age thought transforming traditional systems of belief. While it is easy to say — and in the author's opinion correct — that all that exists is God, God is not limited to anything in particular. God is both transcendent and immanent, both within and without all creation. This means God is both substance and non-substance, both material and non-material. Christ Universal Temple holds that, since all things are pressed from the body of God, there can truly be no evil. This is a strongly Hinduistic kind of belief.

Hindus hold that all things are God, and that good and evil are only the result of divine Maya, or illusion. However, many traditionalists in the Mysteries — and the author as well — believe that God exists in stages of gradient. This means that good and evil are not illusion; they do exist. We believe that God is *all* things, both good and evil. God exists in all things as variations of light and darkness measured upon a single scale. Think of the thermometer and its measure of absolute heat and absolute cold. The boiling point of water is 212°F; the freezing point is 32°F. We understand that these opposing dualities are not measured on two separate scales — a single device is used to measure both extremes and polarities. This is the nature of God.

Another thing taught by Christ Universal Temple is the divinity of man. This is taken right out of a New Age textbook. Traditional Christians (though not necessarily the author) believe this to be the lie of the serpent, regarding the eating of the fruit of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil in the Garden of Eden. The passage reads: "For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil" (Genesis 3:5, NIV). So the serpent told Eve she would surely not die if she ate of the tree, but that her eyes would be opened — knowing good and evil — and that she would become like God. It does seem that this theme permeates the Mysteries across the board.

William Cooper, an early researcher and exposer of what he considered a New Age plot to reveal the Antichrist, stated on his radio show *Hour of the Time*:

> For in the religion of the Mystery Schools, they believe that man was held prisoner in the Garden of Eden by an unjust and vindictive God, and that man was not told by this unjust and vindictive God that he could have the same powers. And man was set free from the bonds of ignorance by Lucifer through his agent, Satan. And many believe that the two are the same, and that's okay, because maybe they are. And that through the gift of intellect man himself will become God. (Cooper, 1994)

This was delivered on episode #441 of the *Hour of the Time* broadcast, originally aired September 16, 1994; Cooper continued his shortwave broadcasts until his death on November 6, 2001. It is obvious from this interpretation why Christians find it so dangerous. The New Age, or Mystery, religions, however, commonly turn it around, holding that Yahweh was in fact an evil God — comparable to the god Enlil in Sumerian mythology — whereas Lucifer, comparable to Lord Enki in Sumerian mythology, is a benevolent God wishing to give mankind the knowledge that would set him free from the bonds of servitude. Enki is said to be mankind's creator, liberator, and savior. It is common for these schools to think of Lucifer either as an Angel of Light or simply as false light. Satan, by contrast, may be thought of as a non-physical, all-pervasive, negative and malignant spiritual presence in the universe. Essentially, Satan is the dark side of God, or the portion of space where God's light does not reside.

The problem with that last view is that if God is omnipresent, as the author believes, then God must be in darkness as well. As outlandish as it sounds, it requires that God must be — now, as he will be in the future — in hell and heaven at the same time. This means that if God is in all places at all times, then God must be all things, both good and evil. There are no limits on omnipotence, omniscience, or omnipresence: "I form the light, and create darkness: I make peace, and create evil: I the LORD do all these things" (Isaiah 45:7, KJV). "Who has spoken and it came to pass, unless the Lord has commanded it? Is it not from the mouth of the Most High that good and bad come?" (Lamentations 3:37–38, ESV). Even the traditional canonical scriptures support such a worldview.

We shall wrap up the study with a consideration and future projection about a possible coming religion, and the state of religion in America and in the world to come. If the New Age practitioners get their way, we will see a fusion of all the world's religions, and a merger of them, at some point in the near future, seemingly unified under one leader. The contention is that this leader will not be Christ as we know him in the West, but rather the one who, they claim, overshadowed Christ — the Master Jesus — when he was on earth. This messiah will be the messiah for all major religions, they assert, the one for whom all the religions wait.

There is no doubt that otherness, separatism, and divisionism among religions cause more problems than good. However, consideration is needed as to why they are there. Jesus himself prayed that his followers would be one — not two or three, but one. Jesus wanted unity, and that is expressed in the biblical texts. The Holy Bible expresses a need for one faith, one hope, one baptism, and so on. We are all to come to a unity of the faith, so that we may attain the full measure and stature of Christ. This is scriptural; and, I might add, it sounds as if we are to become gods — which also happens to be scriptural.

The question lies in the method by which these things will happen. While the Christians say Jesus will literally come in the clouds of heaven, in the sky, the New Agers and others say the world messiah will come in different ways. They may say, for example — as Paul does — that the clouds represent bodies of believers, and that Christ coming in the clouds is consciousness dawning in the individual, the light of Kundalini rising to give birth to the illumined mind. Also, as odd as it may sound, Alice A. Bailey once said that with the advent of airplanes the Christ could now make his second appearance after emerging from his homestead in Tibet and flying to another country to announce his divinity. This is "coming in the clouds" after all, she says (Bailey, 2011).

While many things are doubtful, we must ever be mindful that no one fully knows the secrets of God. Whatever God wishes to do is not based on human understanding. Do the scriptures support the idea of a coming World Religion? Sure: all people worship the Lamb (Jesus) — that is a world religion. Is there a coming World Government? Sure: Jesus rules the nations with an iron rod — that is a world government. So what we believe now will affect what we think will happen in the future. Frankly, what will happen in the future will come regardless of how we perceive or wish things to be.

The most important issue for today is to realize that change is coming, one way or another. That change is something to be feared is only a faulty perception of those under the traditional banner. The only thing certain is change. Change happens. It has always happened, and it always will. The only thing we can say never changes is God — and that is because God is all change and all possibility. He has never not been, so he has never not been what he is, though his manifestations have varied greatly throughout time. At each stage in the development of mankind, we have had different understandings of the nature of God. It is only to be expected that, with each new development of the human species, we will gain greater and greater knowledge.

With this concept in mind, there is no fault in the New Age way of thinking. We are moving out of a period in history marked by very different psychological constructs from the one we are moving into. The advance of technology, the advent of the internet, the development of life-extending methods, and the possible development of sentient machines in the future all place many new thoughts in the mind of the species. Why do we have to be mortal? Why do we have to die? Do we have to die? Many today are working to see to it that death, as we know it — at least for the elite — will become a thing of the past. Does this go against the scriptures? It is possible; but it is also possible that it converges with prophecy and with the scriptures that speak of eternal life, even the resurrection of the dead, which some of the leading scientists intend to make a reality.

We can be certain that nothing which happens in the future will fall outside the plan of the divine providence of almighty God. The author believes that every event that happens, regardless of the meaning we attach to it, is a product of the Prime Mover — God. God is the Mover; God is the cause of all causes. God is not only far removed but also immanent within us. He is far and near, the paradox of all paradoxes. This means that no matter how hard we try to craft our own fate — and though it may seem that we do — it is still playing out through our will as the will of God manifesting through us. We can expect, in the future, that a global leader will emerge, hailed as messiah for all. Let us just hope he is not the menace some in the traditional Christian circles believe he will be:

> A loud sound will be heard world-wide which will herald the coming of Maitreya the Christ. Prompted by this sound, people will be aware of his coming on three distinct levels: spiritual, physical, and emotional.…When Lord Maitreya appears, he will appear as different beings to different people. Men will see him as a man, women will see him as a woman. He will appear as a white to a white, as a black to a black, as an Indian to an Indian, etc. Thus, he will show that he is all things to all people. His three-dimensional image will appear over all nations of the earth simultaneously. He will speak to each ethnic group in their own language. We know this is technologically possible with computer images via telecommunication satellite. (Lambert, 1991)
