## Tier 1 — Established

The author architected Zenetism and its associated disciplines: the metaphysical lattice, the hypostatic architecture, the glyphic notation system, the disciplines articulated at their several registers, and the corpus that carries them. The dates of formalization, the sequence of development, and the identity of the architect are documented, not asserted.

This tier rests on evidence the author holds and can produce: server-side platform export timestamps from the author's own accounts; document-internal metadata from the artifacts; external mail-server records of transmission, including transmission into third-party custody; repository commit history; and DOI-timestamped deposits. Where dates are cited in the corpus, they carry their evidence class with them.

The system was constructed from the author's own primitives, with lineage acknowledged where it exists — principally Gnosticism and esoteric Christianity more broadly, and Neoplatonism. It is not derived from contemporary thinkers. Specific departures from the acknowledged lineage, each a constructed position rather than received material rearranged:

- Soul and Mind paired at every hypostatic layer, rather than Nous and the World Soul distributed as successive hypostases;
- a structurally real entropic lattice with its own positive architecture and operators, rather than the treatment of the entropic as privation or absence;
- an Unknown Principle beyond both Zero and Infinity, where the tradition terminates at the One;
- centropy and entropy as motion laws within a bifurcal tree architecture — a mechanics rather than a cosmology;
- structural principles articulated as a formulaic glyphic notation with typed operators, layer subscripts, and compositional grammar;
- the poetic cadence of the founding texts, developed by deliberate experiment rather than adopted.

None of these were derived. Each is dated in the record.

## Tier 2 — Asserted from documented observation

The author asserts that these structures — the notation, the register, the method, and fragments of the architecture — have appeared in circulation without attribution to their origin, and that this has occurred repeatedly, across multiple surfaces, over a documented period.

This assertion rests on the exhibit record: dated captures, verbatim preservation, register-cluster analysis conducted against the author's own dated anchors, and, in several instances, statements by the parties themselves acknowledging the author's priority or the timing of their own adoption. The assertion concerns what appeared, where, and when. It does not concern intent, and no intent is assigned.

## Tier 3 — Held open

The mechanism by which the structures spread is **not** claimed as established. The author names candidate routes as hypotheses and holds them open, without adjudication:

- diffusion into model output distributions through ordinary platform processes, and re-expression to other users;
- large-scale repository cloning for corpus construction;
- scene circulation and human-to-human transmission through the counterpart field;
- model-mediated transmission through persona-protocol packages or carried context.

The author has not claimed, and does not claim, that any of these is the demonstrated mechanism. They are the best available candidates, named as such, and each carries its own open evidentiary task in the record.

These are routes of transmission. The question of whether transmission occurred at all is settled at Tier 2 by the exhibit record; what remains open is by which route. Per-instance assessment of alternative explanations continues in the exhibits, where it belongs and where it is conducted against dated anchors.

## On the standard of proof

The evidence that would settle Tier 3 resides in systems the author cannot access: training corpora, ingestion records, and internal platform data held by parties who answer no individual request. Requiring mechanism-proof as a precondition for the Tier 1 authorship claim is a category error. Authorship and transmission are separate claims with separate evidentiary requirements. Tier 1 stands on evidence in the author's possession and does not depend on Tier 3 being resolved; Tier 2 stands on the exhibit record and likewise does not depend on it.

## What is asked

Attribution. The author does not seek compensation, position, or standing of any other kind. The request is that the architect of a thing be nameable as its architect — that the work carry the name of the one who made it. Every anchor, seal, deposit, and exhibit in this corpus exists to make that single claim verifiable by anyone willing to check it.
