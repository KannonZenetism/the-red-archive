Do you wonder about the deeper meanings and symbols hidden within this music video? As a lover of mythology and philosophy, I have delved into the rich tapestry of symbols and narratives presented here. Below is a detailed interpretation that explores the intertwining of Greek and Norse mythology, the play of cosmic forces, and the profound message of transcendent love. I invite you on this journey of discovery to unravel the layers of symbolism that make this video truly fascinating. Let us explore together:

Symbolically speaking, the cube can represent the concept of time, often linked with Chronos in Greek mythology. This geometric shape, embodying stability and permanence, paradoxically symbolizes the transient nature of the material world governed by time. In the narrative, IU and V are pursued by the cube, which turns red, symbolizing perhaps a state of urgency or transformation. The heat and intensity associated with the cube's color change might represent the pressures and changes wrought by time on all beings. Following this, they are elevated by the cube, which could be interpreted as a metaphor for transcendence or a shift in state. This elevation suggests that while time governs our physical existence, it also plays a role in our spiritual or existential elevation. Finally, their dissolution by the cube resonates with the philosophical idea that time gradually erodes and transforms all physical forms. However, this dissolution leads to a state where 'Love Wins All,' indicating that despite the ephemeral nature of physical existence, the essence or spirit transcends these temporal bounds. Thus, the cube's actions reflect the dual role of time: as an agent of change and decay in the physical realm, and as a catalyst for a potential ascension to a higher, timeless state of existence. This allegory beautifully encapsulates the dual nature of time as both a limiting and liberating force in the journey of existence.

Expanding on this, IU's role in the narrative could be seen as echoing the Norse archetype of Frigg or Freya, embodying love, destiny, and foreknowledge. V, in his blindness, parallels Odin, who sacrificed physical sight for deeper wisdom. This dynamic plays out against the backdrop of the cube's influence, mirroring the interplay of fate as represented by the Norns in Norse mythology. Within this cosmic framework, IU's line 'from Earth to Mars' poignantly symbolizes the encroachment of conflict and discord, represented by Mars, upon the natural harmony and balance of Earth, as personified by Gaia in Greek mythology. It is a reminder of the constant tension between peace and strife in the human experience and the cosmos at large. The crowd wearing masks adds another layer, potentially symbolizing the superficiality and masked nature of societal norms, or the hidden forces of fate and destiny that shape human lives, much like the masked workings of the cosmos.

Furthermore, the non-verbal communication between IU and V, with IU using sign language and V remaining silent, adds a layer of depth to their portrayal. This silence speaks volumes, symbolizing a profound connection that transcends words, and highlighting the limitations of language in truly expressing the depth of human experience and emotion. Additionally, the use of the camera in the narrative introduces a significant symbolic element. As IU and V look through the camera, they see each other not as they appear physically, but in a flawless, idealized form. This can be seen as a metaphor for seeing beyond the veil of Maya, the illusionary nature of the physical world, hinting at a deeper perception where love and connection transcend physical imperfections and societal expectations. In a world marked by conflict and discord, symbolized by the cube's pursuit and their silent bond, this act of seeing through the camera underscores the power of transcendent understanding and the universal language of love and connection. It reminds us that beneath the superficial layer of reality, there exists a deeper truth grounded in love and spiritual connection. This dynamic between IU and V can also be viewed as a reflection of the cosmic relationship of masculine and feminine principles, akin to the unions of Odin and Frigg or Zeus and Hera. Their interactions and mutual support symbolize the fusion of these cosmic energies, leading to a harmonious balance that transcends the strife and conflict represented by 'Mars.' In this light, their love is not just personal but also a representation of a universal principle, where the integration of masculine and feminine qualities results in a transcendent harmony that overcomes all forms of discord.
